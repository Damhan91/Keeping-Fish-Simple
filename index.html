Introduction

In This page I am going to outline what is needed to set up your very first tropical freshwater aquarium. Throughout this website we will go though the what is needed when setting up your very first freshwater tropical aquarium. We will got throught the equipment that is needed, the best fish for begginers, so you have the best chance of success and also some plants that are not only great for begginers but are also benifical to your aquarium. So lets fist take a look at what you might need to set up your very first aquarium.

What you need to get started

This section will have a small paragraph explaining what is needed.

Location, Location, Location

Picking a location for your aquarium is more important than you think. Try not to have it beside windows or heating or aircon vents. As this will cause algae blooms with the light and temperature fluctuations with the heat and aircon vents

Cleaning/Maintence equipment

Having cleaning and Maintence equipment will make you job as a fish keeper much much easier. We recomend having a gravel syphon, glass cleaner (sponge or magentic), net and a algae remover.

Tank (bigger is better)

Despite what you might think, having a bigger aquarium is more easier to care for than smaller one. The more volume of water you have, the easier it is to maintain and balance your aquarium enviroment. We recomend at lwast 20 gallons (75 liters)

Filter

The filter is an important part of keeping a healty aquarium. This will help clean your water and filter out the fish waste and debris. We recomend either a hang on back filter or internal filter. Just make sure that which ever filter you choose that it is suffiecent enough to deal with the volume of water you have in your aquarium.

Heater and Thermometer

Fish can't produce their own body heat, so its important to keep them at their ideal temperature with a heater. Its also advise to have a thermometer, this will help you keep track on the temperature of the water.

Lighting

If your aquarium comes with an built in light, that great!. Just remeber to have you light on between 8-12 hours per day. Once you get more comfortable with your aquarium, maybe think of upgrading your light. Now, you can get lights with timers and adjustable lighting.

Substrate

There are many types of aquarium substrates, from gravel and stones to sand. Having substrate is a great place for benifical bacteria to live, it also makes you aquarium look more aesthetically pleasing.
